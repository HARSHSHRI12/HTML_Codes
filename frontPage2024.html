is a common Indian surname, primarily associated with the Kayastha community in northern India.

and "vāstava" (meaning "real" or "dweller").
Therefore, it can be interpreted as "one who dwells in prosperity" or "a real or true dweller of prosperity."

and they are often recognized for their contributions to various fields including governance, literature, education, and law.
